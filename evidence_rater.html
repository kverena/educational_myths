Well done – here are the key takeaways

Rating evidence quality is one of the most important skills in academic writing. Here is what this exercise was designed to show:

A single study – even a published one – is much weaker evidence than a systematic review or meta-analysis, which synthesises many studies and controls for individual weaknesses.
Sample size matters: a study with n=12 cannot support strong generalisations. Always ask: how many participants, and from what context?
Correlation is not causation. Many educational studies find associations but cannot prove that one thing caused another.
For your deconstruction chapter: if you rely on only one or two studies, acknowledge their limitations. One strong meta-analysis is worth more than five individual studies cited uncritically.
