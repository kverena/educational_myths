Search for Your Myth / Literatursuche

Theoretical & Disciplinary Grounding — Literature Search Assignment
Universität Passau
⏱ ~30 min individual work / Einzelarbeit ⏱ Plenary / Plenum: ~10 min 📄 Fill in the worksheet as you go / Arbeitsblatt laufend ausfüllen
1
Collect your starting search terms / Erste Suchbegriffe sammeln
Write down 2–3 keywords that describe your myth.
Notiere 2–3 Suchbegriffe.
~ 3 min

2
Run a first search in an appropriate database / Erste Datenbanksuche
Go to eric.ed.gov (or another relevant database). Apply the filter Peer Reviewed. How many results do you get? Does the volume suggest a well-researched area – or a gap?
Gehe zu eric.ed.gov, setze den Filter "Peer Reviewed". Wie viele Treffer? Viel oder wenig Forschung?
~ 7 min
Record here / Hier eintragen
3
Assess what you find – and what it might mean for your argumentation
Look at 3–5 results. For each: What is the main claim? Does it challenge the myth, support it, or nuance it? Note which direction the evidence seems to point – you may need to revise your initial search terms based on what you find.
Lies 3–5 Abstracts. Wohin zeigt die Evidenz? Passe deine Suchbegriffe ggf. an.
~ 10 min

You can't decide?
Maybe you haven't yet fully understood what your myth is actually claiming. Ask: "What exactly does someone believe who holds this myth? What would have to be true for the myth to be right?"
You found more than one route?
A focused argument is stronger than a comprehensive survey (the paper has a page limit ;-) → depth beats breadth.
You find very little?
Maybe your search is too narrow or you haven't found the right terms yet → try switching German terms into appropriate English ones, broaden from a specific subgroup to a more general concept.
You find too much?
Decide for a more specific search strategy and apply filters! One (current) high-quality synthesis is more useful than a lot of individual studies.
4
Reflect on your findings / Befunde reflektieren
Based on what you found: Which direction does the evidence point? What remains open? Is an empirical deconstruction feasible for your myth – or might a different angle be needed?
Wohin zeigt die Evidenz? Was bleibt offen? Ist eine empirische Dekonstruktion möglich?
~ 5 min

5
Prepare one finding for sharing with the seminar / Vorbereitung Plenum
Based on your reflection above, be ready to share: (a) one source and its main claim, (b) what you still can't answer, and (c) whether an empirical deconstruction seems feasible for your myth.
(a) Eine Quelle + Kernaussage, (b) Was bleibt offen?, (c) Ist eine empirische Dekonstruktion möglich?
~ 3 min
Plenary discussion / Plenumsdiskussion (~10 min)
  • What did you find – and what surprised you?
  • Where was the evidence thin, contradictory, or hard to access?
  • Did the search change how you think about your myth?
  • Which myth seems hardest to deconstruct empirically – and why?
